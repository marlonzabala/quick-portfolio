What is SMP

SMP (Scalp micropigmentation) is a cosmetic procedure that is used to give the illusion of thicker hair follicles in balding areas. Alternative to hair plugs or other hair augmentation procedures, the “hair” in SMP treatments is created through tattoo ink. The goal of SMP is to use tiny, layered dots in different hues of black to replicate the look of a shadow on your scalp. This style is referred to as pointillism, and it is utilized to create natural-looking depth and definition on the scalp. Similarly, SMP can be used to cover over scars in the scalp that disrupt the natural pattern of the hair.

Getting your wig covered by insurance:

When filing your claim for insurance coverage, it’s vital that you use the correct terminology and diagnosis codes assigned to you by your prescribing physician when submitting your reimbursement claim.

Some common terms for medical wigs include:

  • cranial prosthesis (CP)
  • hair prosthesis
  • cranial hair prosthesis
  • extra-cranial prosthesis.
